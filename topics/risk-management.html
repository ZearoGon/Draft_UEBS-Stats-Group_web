Research Topic · Finance

Risk Management

Risk management research examines the statistics-based knowledge and quantitative models used to measure, monitor, and mitigate financial risk. This covers market risk, credit risk, operational risk, and emerging areas like climate-related financial risks.

Key Concepts & Methodologies

Risk management relies on rigorous statistical methods to quantify uncertainty and potential losses. The field encompasses both parametric and non-parametric approaches to risk measurement.

Core Areas

  • Value at Risk (VaR) — Parametric, historical simulation, and Monte Carlo approaches
  • Expected Shortfall (CVaR) — Coherent risk measures beyond VaR
  • Volatility Modelling — GARCH family models for conditional heteroskedasticity
  • Extreme Value Theory — Modelling tail risk and rare events
  • Copula Models — Dependency structures for joint risk modelling
  • Stress Testing & Scenario Analysis — Quantifying portfolio risk under extreme conditions
