Research Topic

Econometrics Foundation

Econometrics is a fundamental discipline that bridges economics and statistics. It seeks to provide empirical content to economic theories by applying statistical methods to economic data. Econometric studies aim to quantify economic relationships, test hypotheses, and forecast future trends using real-world data. Statistical models and estimation techniques are intensively used to analyze economic phenomena and inform policy decisions.

Key Concepts & Frameworks

We summarize the important econometric concepts, frameworks, tools and methodologies below. The econometrics section covers a range of fundamental techniques used in empirical research across business disciplines.

Core Areas

  • Ordinary Least Squares (OLS) — The foundational regression technique for estimating linear relationships
  • Instrumental Variables (IV) — Addressing endogeneity through external instruments
  • Panel Data Methods — Fixed and random effects for longitudinal data analysis
  • Maximum Likelihood Estimation (MLE) — Parametric estimation under distributional assumptions
  • Generalized Method of Moments (GMM) — Flexible estimation using moment conditions
  • Time Series Analysis — ARIMA, VAR, and cointegration methods
