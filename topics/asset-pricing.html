Research Topic · Finance

Asset Pricing

Asset Pricing is an important research topic in finance. Asset pricing studies try to answer the fundamental question — whether the return of financial assets (typically stocks, bonds, options and other derivative instruments) can be predicted (usually in cross-section). Statistical models and tests are intensively used to examine the return predictability. We summarize some important statistics-based knowledge and post it below.

Key Concepts & Methodologies

Asset pricing research draws on a rich set of statistical and econometric tools to examine whether expected returns vary across assets and over time. Below are the core frameworks commonly used in the field.

Core Areas

  • CAPM & Factor Models — Capital Asset Pricing Model and multi-factor extensions (Fama-French, Carhart)
  • Fama-MacBeth Regression — Two-pass procedure for testing cross-sectional return predictability
  • GMM-based Tests — Hansen-Jagannathan bounds and stochastic discount factor tests
  • Anomalies & Trading Strategies — Momentum, value, size effects and their statistical significance
  • High-Frequency Econometrics — Realized volatility, microstructure noise, and intraday patterns
  • Machine Learning in Asset Pricing — Prediction with high-dimensional covariates
